# Media Generation

KeyRing AI can organize workflows that involve generated media, including image-oriented and video-oriented model modes where supported by the selected provider. The goal is to let users work with media-capable providers from the same local workspace used for chat, reasoning, attachments, history, and metrics.

Media availability depends heavily on provider support, model access, account tier, regional rollout, safety policies, and pricing. A model appearing in a provider's catalog does not guarantee that a specific account can generate media with it.

## Image Workflows

Image-capable models can be selected through provider and model workflows when the provider exposes image generation or image-related capabilities. Users should start with simple prompts and provider defaults before adding complex options.

Generated images may be displayed, referenced, or exported depending on the workflow. Users should review provider terms for ownership, commercial use, safety restrictions, watermarking, and retention.

## Video Workflows

Video generation is usually job-based and may take longer than a text request. KeyRing AI can show job status such as queued, processing, running, completed, failed, or cancelled. Where available, users can cancel, retry failed jobs, and preview completed results.

Video jobs can be expensive and time-consuming. Before running a large prompt, test with a short, low-risk generation and confirm that the selected provider account supports the model and mode.

## Media In Tool Workflows

Tool-enabled agents may produce or reference generated media as part of a larger task. In those cases, review both the final answer and the generated asset. A text summary may not reveal whether the media output is acceptable, compliant, or on-brand.

## Data And Rights Guidance

Do not submit confidential brand assets, customer likenesses, private media, regulated data, or third-party copyrighted content unless your organization has approved the provider and use case. Provider terms govern how media prompts and generated outputs are handled by that provider.

Keep prompts specific and review outputs before sharing them. Generated media can be inaccurate, biased, policy-limited, or inconsistent across runs.

## Public Boundary

This document describes public media workflow behavior. It does not include proprietary media adapters, internal job storage, private provider keys, source paths, or customer-generated assets.
